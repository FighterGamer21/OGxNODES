About Mount Kailash & Mansarovar

Mount Kailash (6,638 m), located in the far-western region of Tibet, holds immense religious and spiritual significance across Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Bon. Believed to be the abode of Lord Shiva, it is a place of divine power and spiritual transformation.

Beside it lies the sacred Lake Mansarovar (4,590 m), representing purity and divinity. Pilgrims perform holy dips and Parikrama around both the lake and mountain to cleanse their karma and achieve spiritual fulfillment.

The Kailash Yatra is more than just a pilgrimage — it is a deeply spiritual journey requiring devotion, mental strength, and physical endurance amid pristine high-altitude terrain.

Essential Travel & Altitude Information

  • Location: Tibet Autonomous Region, China
  • Mount Kailash Height: 6,638 meters (21,778 ft)
  • Lake Mansarovar Height: 4,590 meters (15,060 ft)
  • Parikrama Length: Approx. 52 km (3 Days)
  • Highest Point: Dolma La Pass – 5,630 meters (18,471 ft)
  • Ideal Visiting Time: May to September
  • Climate: Dry alpine weather, sudden snow possible
  • Avg Day Temp: 5°C – 18°C
  • Avg Night Temp: -5°C or lower
  • Accessible From: Nepal (Simikot or Gyirong) & Mainland China
  • Required Documents: Tibet Travel Permit & Group Chinese Visa
  • Religious Importance: Hindu, Buddhist, Jain & Bon traditions

📘 About Kailash & Mansarovar

Mount Kailash (6,638 meters) in the remote regions of Tibet is regarded as one of the most sacred sites in Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Bon. It is believed to be the divine abode of Lord Shiva, making it a dream pilgrimage for countless devotees.

Just nearby lies Lake Mansarovar (4,590 meters), believed to have been created in the mind of Lord Brahma, symbolizing purity and enlightenment. The entire journey is a spiritual test of endurance and faith.

  • Altitude: Kailash – 6,638m, Mansarovar – 4,590m
